Exam I - Study Guide

Sound

  • What is a periodic signal?
  • What is the period of a sound?
  • What is the wavelength of a sound?
  • What is the frequency of a sound?
  • What is a Hertz?
  • What is the human hearing range in frequency?
  • What is the logarithmic unit of measurement used for measuring amplitude?
  • What is full scale?
  • What is DC Offset?
  • What is a unipolar and bipolar signal?

Digital Audio

  • What is the Sampling Theorem?
  • What is the Nyquist Frequency? Can you calculate it for a given sampling rate?
  • How does bit-depth resolution affect a digital audio signal representation?
  • What is CD Audio sampling rate and bit-depth?
  • What is Aliasing?
  • Could you describe how audio is represented digitally?

Additive Synthesis

  • What is the Time Domain? What is the Frequency Domain?
  • Can you describe Additive Synthesis?
  • If you saw code, could you recognize it as Additive Synthesis?
  • Can you describe what are features and drawbacks of Additive Synthesis?

Subtractive Synthesis

  • Can you describe Subtractive Synthesis?
  • What are the classic waveforms used? Can you describe what harmonics are present and the formula to calculate the strength (amplitude) of those harmonics for each waveform?
  • Besides period sound sources, what other kind of sound source is often used with subtractive synthesis?
  • Could you describe what is a low-pass filter? high-pass? band-pass?
  • Could you explain what is a filter's slope? Or what is the difference between a low-pass filter with -12 and -24dB slope?
  • Could you describe what is a formant?
  • If you saw code, could you recognize it as Subtractive Synthesis?

Modulation

  • Could you describe what is modulation in general?
  • Could you describe what is a low-frequency oscillator and why it is called low-frequency?
  • If we raise the frequency of a low-frequency oscillator, when does it cease to be a low-frequency oscillator and just be an oscillator?
  • What is it called when a low-frequency oscillator is used to modulate amplitude?
  • What is the primary difference between Ring Modulation and Amplitude Modulation?
  • If you saw code, could you recognize it as Ring Modulation? Amplitude Modulation?
  • Could you calculate the sideband frequencies generated with ring modulation?
  • Could you calculate the original carrier/modulator frequencies if you knew a signal was generated with ring modulation?
  • What is it called when a low-frequency oscillator is used to modulate frequency?
  • Do you know what and how many sideband frequencies are generated with Simple FM for a given carrier frequency, modulator frequency, and index?
  • Could you describe what happens with sidebands for Complex FM, particularly the Parallel Modulator -> Single Carrier and Cascade topologies?
  • Could you draw the four Complex FM operator topologies using M and C to represent modulators and carriers, with arrow lines between them to represent the flow of signals?

Samples

  • Could you describe what is sample playback?
  • Could you describe what happens to the frequency content when you speed up or slow down playback by a given rate (i.e., half, double, etc.)?
  • Do you know how long a recorded audio sample will last in time if the speed of playback was double the original rate? Half? Four times?
  • Could you describe two different approaches to sample playback?
  • Could you explain what are loop points and how they are used in instrumental sample playback?
  • If you were given a sample of an instrument and told its fundamental frequency and you wanted to play it back at a desired frequency, could you give the calculation (i.e., ratio) for the appropriate speed of playback to get the desired frequency?
